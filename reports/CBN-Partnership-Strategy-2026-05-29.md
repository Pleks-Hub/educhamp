**Risk:** Partner firm doesn't understand ASA's governance frameworks or delivery model.  
**Mitigation:**
- Early alignment meeting (before EOI submission)
- Shared SOW + delivery schedule
- Weekly sync calls during delivery
- Clear role separation (partner = CBN relationship, ASA = technical delivery)

### Risk 2: CBN Scope Creep
**Risk:** CBN asks for work outside ISO 42001 + AI governance (e.g., custom AI development).  
**Mitigation:**
- Clear scope statement in EOI + full RFP
- Change order process (any scope expansion = additional fee)
- Phase gates (no Phase 2 until Phase 1 complete + approved)

### Risk 3: Timeline Pressure
**Risk:** CBN wants faster delivery; we slip on quality.  
**Mitigation:**
- Realistic timeline from day 1 (18 months is tight but doable with 3+ FTE)
- Buffer contingency in plan (show 19 months, deliver in 18)
- Early team mobilization (start recruiting Phase 1 team in June)

### Risk 4: Competitive Bid
**Risk:** Another firm offers similar services at lower cost.  
**Mitigation:**
- Position on depth (ISO 42001 auditor, not generic AI training)
- Emphasize implementation risk if competitor has no governance background
- Offer quick-win early phase (Phase 1 can start before contract fully signed)

### Risk 5: Partner Firm Capacity
**Risk:** Partner firm doesn't have bandwidth to manage CBN relationship while we deliver.  
**Mitigation:**
- Assign dedicated partner liaison (one person, not rotating)
- Weekly partner-ASA syncs (so partner knows status before CBN asks)
- Escalation path (if partner-CBN friction, ASA speaks to CBN directly)
