**Question:** A baker uses a ratio of 3 cups of sugar to 4 cups of flour for a cake. If she wants to make a larger cake that requires 18 cups of flour, how many cups of sugar will she need? What is the total number of cups of ingredients (sugar and flour) for the larger cake?

**Options:**
- **A.** 13.5 cups of sugar, 31.5 total cups ✓
- **B.** 24 cups of sugar, 42 total cups
- **C.** 13.5 cups of sugar, 27 total cups
- **D.** 24 cups of sugar, 37.5 total cups

**Correct Answer:** A

**Explanation:** The ratio of sugar to flour is 3:4. We need to find an equivalent ratio where the flour is 18 cups. To go from 4 cups of flour to 18 cups of flour, we multiply by 18/4 = 4.5. So, we multiply the sugar by 4.5 as well: 3 cups sugar * 4.5 = 13.5 cups of sugar. The total number of cups of ingredients for the larger cake is 13.5 cups of sugar + 18 cups of flour = 31.5 cups.
